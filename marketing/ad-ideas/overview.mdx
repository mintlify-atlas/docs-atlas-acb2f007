## What's inside

This hub captures Genspark chat outputs on advertising ideation. It covers how to generate strong creative concepts, how to evaluate them before spending money, and how to think about campaign strategy across paid channels.

## Creative concept development

Ad creative is the primary variable in paid advertising performance. These frameworks address how to generate, evaluate, and iterate on ad concepts systematically rather than relying on inspiration.

**Topics covered:**

- Concept generation — structured approaches to ideating ad creative across different angles (problem, transformation, social proof, curiosity, contrarian)
- Angle selection — identifying which creative angle is most likely to resonate with a specific audience at a specific stage of awareness
- Headline and hook writing — the first line of an ad determines whether anyone reads the rest; patterns for high-performing hooks
- Visual direction — how written concepts translate into visual briefs for designers or video teams
- Iteration frameworks — how to systematically test and evolve creative based on performance data

## Campaign strategy

Individual ad concepts live inside campaigns. These frameworks address the broader campaign structure that determines how creative performs.

**Topics covered:**

- Audience targeting strategy — matching creative angles to audience segments at different awareness levels
- Funnel mapping — which ad types and messages belong at awareness, consideration, and conversion stages
- Budget allocation — how to distribute spend across campaigns and when to scale
- Testing methodology — A/B testing principles applied to ad creative, copy, and targeting
- Retargeting — how to structure retargeting sequences that move prospects toward conversion without being intrusive

## Ad formats and channels

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Social ads">
    Meta (Facebook/Instagram), LinkedIn, and TikTok ads each have distinct creative conventions. Social ads interrupt — they appear in feeds where users are not actively searching. Creative must earn attention before delivering a message.

    Key principles:
    - Lead with a pattern interrupt (visual or copy-based)
    - Make the value proposition clear within the first 3 seconds (video) or first line (static)
    - Match the ad's aesthetic to the platform's native content style
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Search ads">
    Google and Bing search ads reach people who are actively looking for a solution. Creative here is primarily about relevance and credibility rather than interruption.

    Key principles:
    - Mirror the language of the search query in the headline
    - Lead with the strongest benefit or differentiator
    - Use ad extensions to increase real estate and provide additional entry points
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Video ads">
    Pre-roll, mid-roll, and YouTube ads have a unique challenge: viewers can skip. The hook is everything.

    Key principles:
    - Deliver the core message in the first 5 seconds (before the skip option appears)
    - Use the skip as a qualifier — people who watch past it are genuinely interested
    - End with a single, clear call to action
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Ideation process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define the audience and awareness level">
    Before generating a single concept, be specific about who you're speaking to and how much they already know about your offer. Cold audiences need different creative than warm retargeting audiences.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Identify the strongest angle">
    Generate 5–10 different angles (problem-focused, transformation-focused, social proof, curiosity-driven, contrarian). Pick the 2–3 most likely to resonate based on what you know about your audience.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write multiple concepts per angle">
    Don't commit to the first idea. Write 3–5 executions of each angle. The best concept is rarely the obvious one.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Evaluate against creative criteria">
    Before testing, filter concepts through three questions: Is the hook strong enough to stop the scroll? Is the value proposition clear? Is the call to action specific and low-friction?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test systematically">
    Run structured tests. Change one variable at a time. Let data eliminate concepts rather than opinion.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>Most ad creative fails because it tries to say too much. Pick one idea, one audience, one action. Clarity outperforms cleverness in paid advertising.</Tip>
