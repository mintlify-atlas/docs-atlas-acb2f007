## Overview

The EOL (End-of-Life) hub holds projects that have been sunset or deprecated. These are initiatives that were actively worked on but are no longer pursued — either because the direction changed, the market shifted, or the project simply didn't move forward. Keeping EOL work indexed prevents knowledge loss and helps inform future decisions.

## Why preserve EOL work

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Avoid repeating mistakes">
    EOL projects document what was tried and didn't work. Reviewing them before starting similar work prevents repeating the same experiments.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Recover reusable elements">
    Even projects that didn't succeed often contain frameworks, research, or copy that can be repurposed.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Maintain institutional memory">
    As time passes, the reasoning behind past decisions fades. EOL archives preserve context that would otherwise be lost.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Status
