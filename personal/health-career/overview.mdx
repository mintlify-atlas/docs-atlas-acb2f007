## Key topics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Clinical vs. non-clinical roles">
    Health careers span a wide spectrum from direct patient care (medicine, nursing, physical therapy) to non-clinical roles (health coaching, corporate wellness, health technology, medical writing). Research covers how to identify which pathway fits your goals, background, and lifestyle preferences.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Health and wellness coaching">
    Health coaching is a growing field with increasing demand. Research covers the difference between health coaching and clinical practice, reputable certification programs (NBC-HWC, ACE, NASM), scope of practice, and how to build a client base.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Nutrition credentials">
    Nutrition career paths range from registered dietitian (RD/RDN) — requiring a master's degree, supervised practice, and board exam — to certified nutrition coaches with shorter certification programs. Research covers the differences in scope, credibility, and how to choose the right credential for your goals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Personal training and fitness careers">
    Personal training certifications vary widely in rigor and recognition. Research covers leading certifications (NASM, ACE, NSCA, ACSM), how to differentiate in a competitive market, and pathways into sports performance, group fitness, and online coaching.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Health technology and digital health">
    Digital health is one of the fastest-growing sectors at the intersection of healthcare and technology. Research covers roles in health tech (product management, clinical informatics, UX for health apps), required skills, and how to transition into this space from a clinical or non-clinical background.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Building a wellness business">
    Research covers the fundamentals of launching a wellness practice or coaching business: legal structure, pricing, client acquisition, content marketing, and how to navigate the regulatory landscape for health-related services.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Career exploration by pathway

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Clinical">
    Clinical paths — medicine, nursing, physical therapy, occupational therapy, pharmacy, dietetics — require formal degree programs, clinical hours, and licensure. They offer strong employment stability and clear scope of practice. Research covers how to evaluate programs, understand licensing requirements, and navigate career transitions within clinical settings.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Wellness coaching">
    Wellness coaching is accessible with shorter training timelines and lower upfront investment. The field lacks universal licensure, so certification quality and niche specialization matter significantly for credibility. Research covers how to choose a certification, define a niche, and build a sustainable coaching practice.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Health technology">
    Health technology roles blend healthcare knowledge with technical or business skills. Entry points include clinical informatics, health data analysis, product management at health tech companies, and UX research for digital health tools. Prior clinical experience is valued but not always required.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Entrepreneurship">
    Health entrepreneurs build businesses around their expertise — coaching practices, digital products, content platforms, or wellness services. Research covers business model options, how to validate a niche, pricing strategy, and the compliance considerations specific to health-related businesses.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>Scope of practice rules vary by jurisdiction and credential type. Always verify what you are legally permitted to do in your location before offering health or nutrition advice professionally.</Note>
