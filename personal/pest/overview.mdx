## Overview

Pest control strategies, prevention methods, and natural solutions. This hub collects Genspark research on how to identify, manage, and prevent common household pests — with an emphasis on integrated pest management (IPM) approaches that minimize chemical exposure.

## Key topics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Integrated pest management (IPM)">
    IPM is the gold standard approach to pest control. It prioritizes prevention, monitoring, and targeted interventions over routine pesticide application. Research covers the IPM framework, how to apply it in a home setting, and why it is more effective long-term than chemical-only approaches.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ants">
    Ant control starts with finding and eliminating food and water sources, then sealing entry points. Bait-based treatments are more effective than sprays for most ant species because they allow workers to carry toxicant back to the colony. Research covers the most effective baits for common species and how to identify the entry trail.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cockroaches">
    Cockroaches are resilient and reproduce rapidly, making early intervention important. Gel baits (containing fipronil or indoxacarb) are the most effective consumer products. Research covers how to deploy bait correctly, why spray products are largely counterproductive, and how to seal harborage areas.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Rodents">
    Mice and rats can enter through gaps as small as a dime (mice) or quarter (rats). Research covers exclusion as the primary strategy, snap trap placement and technique, and why poison baits create secondary hazards for pets and wildlife.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Bed bugs">
    Bed bugs are among the most difficult household pests to eliminate. Research covers how to confirm an infestation, heat treatment vs. chemical treatment, mattress encasements, and the importance of treating the entire affected area systematically.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Fruit flies and drain flies">
    These small flies are a nuisance pest usually solved by eliminating breeding sites — overripe fruit, damp organic matter in drains, and standing water. Research covers how to locate the source, DIY traps, and drain treatments that actually work.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Prevention fundamentals

<Steps>
  <Step title="Eliminate food and water sources">
    Store food in sealed containers, fix leaks promptly, keep drains clean, and avoid leaving pet food out overnight. Most pests enter homes in search of food and water — removing these attracts fewer pests in the first place.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Seal entry points">
    Inspect the exterior of your home for gaps around pipes, utility lines, doors, windows, and the foundation. Use caulk, expanding foam, steel wool, or hardware cloth depending on the gap type and location.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reduce harborage areas">
    Clutter inside and debris outside give pests places to hide and nest. Keep storage areas organized, reduce cardboard boxes (cockroaches love them), and maintain a clear perimeter around the home's exterior.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Monitor regularly">
    Sticky traps placed in corners, under appliances, and along walls help you detect pest activity before it becomes a full infestation. Check them every 1–2 weeks.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act at first signs">
    A small pest problem is dramatically easier to resolve than an established infestation. Act promptly when you see the first signs — droppings, damage, or live insects.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Natural solutions that work">
    - **Diatomaceous earth** — effective against crawling insects with exoskeletons (ants, cockroaches, bed bugs) when applied as a thin dust in undisturbed areas
    - **Boric acid** — slow-acting stomach poison effective for cockroaches and ants when applied as a fine dust in hidden areas
    - **Peppermint oil** — some evidence as a deterrent for mice; less effective as a primary control method
    - **Cedar** — repels some fabric pests (moths) in enclosed spaces like closets
    - **Vinegar** — disrupts ant scent trails temporarily but does not kill colony; use as a wipe-down between treatments
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="What does not work">
    - Ultrasonic repellers — no reliable scientific evidence of effectiveness for any pest
    - Dryer sheets as mouse repellent — not supported by evidence
    - Cucumber peel for ants — anecdotal at best
    - Essential oils as primary cockroach control — insufficient for established infestations
    - Spraying pesticide without finding the source — kills exposed insects but does not address the root cause
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Warning>Some pesticide products are harmful to children, pets, and beneficial insects. Always read labels carefully, follow application instructions, and store products safely. When in doubt, consult a licensed pest management professional.</Warning>
