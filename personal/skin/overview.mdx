## Key topics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="The core skincare framework">
    Effective skincare starts with three non-negotiables: cleansing, moisturizing, and sun protection. Research covers how to execute these basics well before adding active ingredients or treatments.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Retinoids and vitamin A">
    Retinoids (retinol, tretinoin, adapalene) are among the most researched skincare ingredients. Research covers how they work, how to introduce them without irritation, and realistic expectations for results.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Sun protection">
    UV exposure is the primary driver of photoaging and skin cancer risk. Research covers SPF selection, broad-spectrum protection, how much to apply, and reapplication strategies for everyday use.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Skin barrier health">
    A compromised skin barrier leads to sensitivity, dehydration, and inflammation. Research covers ingredients that support barrier function (ceramides, fatty acids, cholesterol) and habits that damage it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Diet and skin health">
    Internal factors — nutrition, hydration, sleep, and stress — significantly affect skin appearance. Research covers the evidence for diet's role in acne, aging, and overall skin quality.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Skincare by concern

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Acne">
    Acne is driven by excess sebum, clogged pores, bacteria, and inflammation. Evidence-based topical ingredients include benzoyl peroxide, salicylic acid, niacinamide, and adapalene. Avoiding pore-clogging products and maintaining a consistent routine are foundational steps.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Hyperpigmentation">
    Dark spots and uneven tone respond to sun protection (critical), vitamin C, niacinamide, azelaic acid, and retinoids. Consistent use over months is required — most active ingredients take 8–12 weeks to show visible results.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Aging skin">
    Anti-aging priorities: daily SPF, retinoids, antioxidants, and peptides. Collagen-supporting ingredients show the strongest evidence. Hydration maintains plumpness. Results accumulate over years of consistent use.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Sensitive skin">
    Sensitive skin benefits from a minimal routine with fragrance-free, gentle formulations. Focus on barrier repair with ceramides and avoid layering too many actives. Patch testing new products reduces the risk of reactions.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>Dermatology-grade treatments (prescription retinoids, chemical peels, laser) are outside the scope of over-the-counter skincare. A dermatologist can assess conditions that do not respond to self-directed care.</Note>
