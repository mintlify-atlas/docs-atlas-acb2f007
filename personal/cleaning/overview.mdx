## Key topics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Building a cleaning routine">
    Effective cleaning routines are sustainable, not perfect. Research covers how to design a tiered system — quick daily resets, weekly deep zones, and monthly or seasonal tasks — that prevents buildup without requiring marathon cleaning sessions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="High-impact cleaning targets">
    Some areas deliver disproportionate returns: the kitchen sink and counters, bathroom surfaces, floors in high-traffic areas, and anything visible from the front door. Research covers how to prioritize these areas for maximum perceived cleanliness with minimum time.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cleaning products that work">
    Many cleaning tasks require nothing more than a good all-purpose cleaner, microfiber cloths, and a mop. Research covers which products are worth the cost, where natural alternatives (vinegar, baking soda) work well, and what to avoid mixing for safety.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Decluttering frameworks">
    Methods like KonMari (keep what sparks joy), minimalism, and the "one in, one out" rule offer different approaches to reducing possessions. Research covers how to choose a framework that fits your personality, how to start without feeling overwhelmed, and how to handle sentimental items.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Organization systems">
    Good organization makes cleaning faster. Research covers how to group like items, use vertical storage, create zones for different activities, and label systems that other household members will actually follow.
  </Accordion>
      <Accordion title="Speed cleaning techniques">
    When time is limited, a structured speed cleaning approach can produce significant results in 20–30 minutes. Research covers top-to-bottom, room-by-room workflows, how to eliminate decision fatigue by working in a fixed sequence, and which tasks to skip when time is short.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Cleaning routine template

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Daily (10–15 min)">
    - Make the bed
    - Wipe kitchen counters and stovetop after cooking
    - Load/unload the dishwasher
    - Do a quick reset of main living areas — return items to their places
    - Wipe bathroom sink and counter
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Weekly (1–2 hrs)">
    - Vacuum and mop all floors
    - Clean bathrooms fully (toilet, tub/shower, mirror, floor)
    - Change bed linens
    - Clean kitchen appliances (microwave, stovetop, exterior of oven)
    - Dust surfaces and ceiling fans
    - Empty all trash
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Monthly">
    - Clean inside the oven and refrigerator
    - Wipe down cabinet fronts
    - Clean baseboards, door frames, and light switches
    - Descale appliances (kettle, coffee maker, showerhead)
    - Launder throw blankets and decorative pillows
    - Declutter one area or drawer
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Tip>The fastest way to keep a home clean is to deal with messes immediately — wipe spills as they happen, put things back after using them, and do a 5-minute reset before bed each night. Prevention costs far less time than remediation.</Tip>
