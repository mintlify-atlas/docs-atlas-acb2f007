## Training principles

<Steps>
  <Step title="Establish your baseline">
    Assess your current fitness level before designing a program. Knowing your starting point helps you set realistic goals and choose appropriate starting loads and volumes.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply progressive overload">
    Gradually increase the demand on your body over time — more weight, more reps, more sets, or less rest. This is the primary driver of adaptation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Prioritize recovery">
    Adaptation happens during rest, not during training. Adequate sleep, nutrition, and rest days are not optional — they are part of the program.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Track and adjust">
    Log your workouts, monitor how your body responds, and adjust variables when progress stalls. Consistency over months and years produces results.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>Consistency beats perfection. A sustainable routine you follow for years outperforms an optimal program you abandon after weeks.</Tip>
