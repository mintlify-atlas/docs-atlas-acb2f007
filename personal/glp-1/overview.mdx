## Overview

GLP-1 receptor agonists are a class of medications that have transformed the treatment of type 2 diabetes and obesity. This hub collects Genspark research on how these medications work, how they are used, and what the evidence says about metabolic health outcomes.

## Key topics

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Medication classes and examples">
    GLP-1 medications include semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy, Rybelsus), liraglutide (Victoza, Saxenda), dulaglutide (Trulicity), and tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound — a dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist). Research covers the differences between these agents in terms of efficacy, dosing schedules, and side effect profiles.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Side effects and tolerability">
    Common side effects include nausea, vomiting, and gastrointestinal discomfort, especially when starting or increasing doses. Research covers strategies to manage side effects, titration schedules, and when to pause or discontinue.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Diet and lifestyle while on GLP-1s">
    These medications reduce appetite but do not replace the need for nutritional quality. Research covers protein intake targets, resistance training to preserve muscle mass, and how to maintain results if medication is discontinued.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Cost, access, and insurance">
    GLP-1 medications can be expensive without coverage. Research includes strategies for accessing these medications, manufacturer discount programs, compounding pharmacy considerations, and how coverage varies by indication.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Key considerations

<Warning>GLP-1 medications are prescription drugs. Nothing in this knowledge base constitutes medical advice. Consult a qualified healthcare provider before starting, changing, or stopping any medication.</Warning>

<Tip>Pairing GLP-1 therapy with resistance training and adequate protein intake helps preserve lean muscle mass during weight loss — a common concern that research consistently highlights.</Tip>
